This Privacy Policy describes how Skystra ("we," "us," or "the app") collects, uses, and shares your information when you use the Skystra mobile application, including during its TestFlight beta period. By creating an account or using Skystra you agree to the practices described below. If you do not agree, please do not use the app.

Who we are

Skystra is operated by Kyle Greer ("we"), an individual developer based in the United States. You can reach us at wilkylegreer@gmail.com for any privacy question, request, or complaint.

Summary (for the impatient)

  • We collect what's needed to run a small social photo app: account identity, the photos and captions you post, where you took them, and basic device info to deliver notifications and fix crashes.
  • You cannot publish a post without sharing your precise location. Posting a photo records the exact latitude and longitude attached to that photo.
  • Public accounts (the default): posts, profiles, follows, and reactions on public accounts are readable through our backend with no Skystra account required, including by people who do not have an account.
  • Private accounts: if you turn on Private account in Settings, your posts become readable only to followers you've approved, and new follow attempts at you are routed through a request-and-accept flow. The Settings toggle labeled "Show location on posts" still does not take effect.

Information We Collect

Account information

When you create a Skystra account using an email address and password we collect your email and a salted password hash (passwords are never stored in plain text). If you sign in with Sign in with Apple, Apple shares with us a unique account identifier and either your real email or an Apple-generated private relay address, based on your choice; you may also optionally share your name on first sign-in. We use this information solely to create and authenticate your account. You may also optionally provide a display name, profile photo, biographical details, and a chosen unit of distance.

Content you create

Skystra stores the photos, captions, and event details you publish through the app, along with the date and time each photo was captured. This content is associated with your account.

Location data

If you grant the iOS "When in Use" location permission, Skystra reads your device's current latitude and longitude. We use your location for three things:

  • Attaching coordinates to posts you create. When you publish a post, the precise latitude and longitude (as decimal degrees) and a reverse-geocoded "City, State" label are saved to the post row. (The events feature, which would attach the same information to events you create, is not enabled in this public-beta build — see the summary above.)
  • Showing nearby posts on the Discover tab. Your location is sent to a database function (posts_within_radius) that returns posts within a radius you control. The function does not store your query; only the resulting list of posts is returned to your device.
  • Local golden-hour calculations. The app computes the next sunrise and sunset for your current location on-device to schedule the next local notification.

Location is effectively required to publish a post. The composer blocks publishing when location permission is denied. You can still browse, follow accounts, and react to posts without granting location, but you cannot capture and share a new photo.

Coordinates are stored at full precision. Each post row in our database stores a float8 latitude and longitude (approximately ten decimal places of precision, or sub-meter accuracy on the surface of the earth). See How your content is shared below for who can read those coordinates today.

Camera and photo library access

If you grant permission, Skystra accesses your camera so you can capture sunrise and sunset photos in the app, and your photo library so you can attach a recently-taken photo or save a captured photo back. We only access photos you explicitly select or capture in the app; we do not scan your library in the background.

Push notification tokens

If you enable push notifications, your device generates a token issued by Apple. We may store this token, associated with your account, to deliver notifications to your device. The token does not by itself identify you to anyone other than us and Apple.

As of the date above, we do not send any remote push notifications from our servers. Device-level alerts that arrive on your lock screen are scheduled locally on-device (next sunrise, next sunset, 30 minutes before an event you've RSVP'd to). We may add remote push later in the beta period; this policy will be updated before that change ships.

In-app notifications inbox

Separately from device alerts, Skystra has an in-app notifications inbox — the bell icon on the Feed tab. The inbox is backed by a server-side table that holds one row per notification you've received. Rows are inserted automatically by the database (not by the client) when:

  • Someone follows you, or — if your account is private — requests to follow you.
  • A private account whose follow you previously requested accepts the request.
  • Someone reacts to one of your posts. Reactions on the same post with the same reaction type collapse to a single inbox row that tracks the count and the three most-recent actors, so a popular sunrise doesn't generate thirty separate entries.
  • Someone you follow publishes a post.

Each row stores the recipient (you), the actor, the kind of event, optional references to the post or event in question, and read / created / updated timestamps. You can read your own rows only — row-level security on the table denies access to anyone else's inbox, and the client cannot insert rows directly; only the server-side triggers and RPCs can. You can mark rows read, dismiss them, or mark the entire inbox read; dismissed rows are deleted permanently.

A separate notification preferences row, if it exists for your account, records the categories of notifications you've opted out of (per-kind toggles for follow events, reactions, and followee posts). Rows in this table are created lazily — if you haven't changed any defaults, no row exists.

The events feature is not enabled in this public-beta build, so the "someone you follow created an event" notification kind is wired but dormant — no rows are produced until events are re-introduced. This policy will be updated before that happens.

Diagnostic data via TestFlight

While Skystra is distributed through TestFlight, crash logs and basic diagnostic data are provided to us through Apple's TestFlight service and are subject to Apple's Privacy Policy. This data does not personally identify you to us.

We do not embed third-party analytics or advertising SDKs (no Google Analytics, no Facebook Pixel, no AppsFlyer, no Mixpanel, no Sentry, no Crashlytics).

Feedback you submit

If you tap Send feedback in Settings, we collect:

  • The category you picked (Bug / Feature idea / Thoughts)
  • The message you typed (up to 4,000 characters)
  • An optional reply email, if you provided one
  • Your app version and build number, your iOS version, your device hardware model identifier (e.g. iPhone15,3), and your current device locale (e.g. en_US)
  • The user ID of the account submitting the feedback
  • The submission timestamp

The in-app feedback form tells you, in its intro line, that your device and app version are attached automatically. The hardware identifier and locale are part of that device context; we capture them so we can reproduce bugs without asking you. Feedback rows are not readable from the app — only the developer can view them through the Supabase dashboard. If you delete your account, your feedback rows are kept but their user_id is cleared (set to NULL).

Account switcher / keychain

If you sign into more than one Skystra account on the same device, the app remembers each account's access token and refresh token in the iOS keychain so you can switch between them without re-typing your password. Tokens are stored with the kSecAttrAccessibleAfterFirstUnlockThisDeviceOnly attribute, which means:

  • The tokens are not synced to iCloud and do not restore onto a different device when you migrate to a new phone.
  • The tokens are readable only while the device has been unlocked at least once since reboot.

You can remove a stored account from your device by long-pressing its row in the account switcher (long-press the You tab to open it) and tapping Remove from this device. Removing the account from the switcher does not delete the underlying Skystra account — see Your rights and choices for account deletion.

  • Communicate with you about your account or important changes to the service.
  • Triage and fix bugs (especially during the beta period) using the feedback you submit and the crash data Apple shares with us.

We do not:

  • Use your content or behavior for advertising, ad targeting, or third-party analytics.
  • Run user-tracking SDKs from third parties.
  • Use your photos to train a machine learning model.
  • Send your data to a generative-AI service or large language model.

Because we do not track you across apps or websites owned by other companies, the app currently does not prompt for App Tracking Transparency permission, and our App Store privacy nutrition label reports tracking as "Not Used."

How Your Content Is Shared

Skystra is a social application. By design, certain information is visible to others — and during this stage of the beta, "others" includes the public internet, not only signed-in Skystra users. What is readable depends on whether your account is public or private.

Public accounts (the default)

The following data is publicly readable through our Supabase backend, with no requirement that the reader hold a Skystra account:

  • Your profile — username, display name, profile photo, bio.
  • Your posts — photos, captions, sunrise/sunset type, the reverse-geocoded "City, State" label, the precise latitude and longitude where the photo was taken, the time captured, and the time posted.
  • Your social graph — who you follow and who follows you.
  • Your reactions — which posts you have reacted to and with which reaction.

Anyone who knows the publicly published URL of our backend service, and is willing to query it, can read the items above. Please treat anything you post on a public account as public on the open internet.

Private accounts

If you enable Private account in Settings:

  • Your posts become readable only to you and to followers whose follow request you have approved. Anonymous viewers and signed-in Skystra users who do not follow you cannot read the post row, the photo stored in it, or the coordinates attached to it. This is enforced at the database layer (row-level security), not only in the in-app UI.
  • Pending follow requests do not grant visibility. Someone who has requested to follow you sees your posts only after you tap Accept; until then they see what an unrelated viewer sees.
  • Your profile, follow list, and reactions remain publicly readable. The private flag currently gates posts only. We plan to tighten profile and follow-list visibility in a future update, and this policy will be revised when that lands.
  • Switching back to public auto-accepts pending requests. When your account flips from private to public, every pending follow request targeting you becomes an accepted follow in the same database transaction, the requesters are notified, and the pending-request rows are deleted. Going public → private is a one-way change for future follow attempts only — followers you have already approved continue to see your posts until you remove them.

About follow requests

When you tap Follow on a private account, the server records a follow request — a row identifying the requester (you), the requestee (the target account), and the time the request was submitted. Follow-request rows are readable only by the two accounts involved; nobody else can list pending requests at or from another user. Approved requests move to the regular follows table and the pending row is deleted; rejected requests are silently dropped, with no notification sent to the requester that they were rejected (only the visible state change from "Requested" back to "Follow"). Cancelling a pending request from your side deletes the row immediately.

The events feature

The events feature (event details, attendee lists, RSVPs) is not enabled in this public-beta build, so there are no event rows to be exposed to other users from your activity in the app. The server-side hooks that would notify your followers of a new event are wired but dormant; they begin producing rows when the events surface returns. This policy will be updated before that happens.

About the audience picker

The composer for new posts currently displays a picker labeled Everyone / Friends / Only Me. As of the date above, your per-post selection has no effect — the field is not yet stored on the post and every post is treated as "Everyone." We are leaving the control visible because the implementation work is in progress, but you should assume your selection is ignored until this policy says otherwise. Account-level privacy (Settings → Privacy → Private account) does take effect — see the section above.

About the Settings toggles

Settings → Privacy contains two switches:

  • Private account. Now wired through to the backend. Turning it on flips the is_private flag on your profile row, which (a) routes new follow attempts through the request-and-accept flow described above and (b) restricts your posts to approved followers. Profile, follow-list, and reaction visibility are unchanged by this toggle.
  • Show location on posts. Does not currently hide the "City, State" label or the underlying coordinates on posts you publish. It is a local-only preference stored on your device, and we plan to wire it through in a future update.

Deleting a post

You can delete any of your own posts from the app. Deleting a post removes the row from our database; cached copies in other people's feeds will clear on their next refresh.

Sharing Content Outside Skystra

Skystra includes a Share button on the post-success screen that lets you send the photo you just captured, along with a skystra://post/… deep link back to its post, to other apps on your device — for example Messages, Mail, or Instagram. Once content leaves Skystra through the iOS share sheet, what happens to it is governed by the receiving app's privacy practices, not ours.

Map and Location-Search Providers

When you use map or location-search features within Skystra, those interactions are handled by Apple's MapKit and Core Location services. Map tile requests and place lookups are processed by Apple under Apple's Privacy Policy and are not transmitted to our servers in personally identifying form.

How and Where We Store Your Data

Skystra uses Supabase (operated by Supabase, Inc.) as its backend infrastructure provider for authentication, the Postgres database that stores account, post, follow, follow-request, reaction, event, notification, notification-preference, and feedback rows, and the object storage bucket that holds uploaded photos. Supabase acts as a data processor on our behalf. You can review Supabase's privacy practices at https://supabase.com/privacy.

Data in transit between the app and Supabase is encrypted with HTTPS. Data at rest on Supabase is encrypted using standard industry practices.

We do not run our own servers and we do not currently transfer your data to any other processor, with the exception of Apple (Sign in with Apple, MapKit, TestFlight crash reports, and APNs push delivery), whose services are governed by Apple's own privacy policy.

Retention

  • Account row and profile fields — retained while your account exists.
  • Posts, photos, events, follows, reactions, RSVPs — retained while your account exists, or until you delete them individually.
  • Pending follow requests — retained until you cancel them, the target accepts (the row becomes a follow), the target rejects (silently deleted), or either account is deleted.
  • Notifications inbox rows — retained until you dismiss them from the inbox, the underlying subject (post, event, account) is deleted, or your account is deleted. There is no automatic age-based purge.
  • Notification preferences — retained while your account exists; if you haven't changed any defaults, no row exists.
  • Feedback submissions — retained indefinitely so we can revisit past reports against newer builds. If you delete your account, the user_id on your feedback rows is cleared so the rows become anonymous.
  • Keychain tokens for the account switcher — retained on the device until you remove the account from the switcher, sign out from that account explicitly, or uninstall the app.
  • TestFlight crash data — retained by Apple under Apple's retention rules.
  • Beta data wipes. Because Skystra is in active beta development, we may reset the database (deleting all accounts, posts, follows, follow requests, reactions, events, RSVPs, notifications, notification preferences, and feedback) between builds without prior notice. The in-app Beta notice shown to each new account on first launch makes this explicit. If you want to keep a copy of a photo you posted, please save it from your camera roll — we cannot restore wiped beta content.

Your Rights and Choices
